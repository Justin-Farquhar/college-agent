# App Name

College Agent

## One-Sentence Summary

College Agent helps students and families compare colleges like a travel site compares hotels, using real cost, debt, and earnings outcomes to estimate ROI and identify better value options.

## The Problem

Picking a college is one of the biggest financial decisions most people ever make, but the information is scattered and hard to compare apples to apples.

Most students can see sticker price, but struggle to answer the questions that actually matter financially:
How much will I really pay after aid
How much debt do students take on at this school
What do graduates tend to earn after graduation and over time
Is School A actually a better deal than School B for my situation

Right now, people rely on rankings, anecdotes, and marketing. College Agent turns college selection into a clearer financial comparison, similar to how online travel agencies made it easy to compare hotels on price and value.

Long term, College Agent expands from school to school comparisons into program and major level comparisons, for example Mechanical Engineering at UD versus Chemical Engineering at Rutgers.

## User Types

- Guest users (browse and compare without an account)
- Registered users (save schools, save comparisons, set preferences like budget and debt tolerance)

## Core Features

- Search and filter colleges by location, price, debt, and outcomes
- Compare two or more colleges side by side on net price, debt, and earnings metrics
- Estimate ROI and break even timeline using transparent assumptions
- Save a shortlist and favorite comparisons for later
- Share a comparison link or export a simple summary

## What Users See

### Screen 1: Search and Results
A search bar and filters (state, public vs private, net price range, debt range, completion rate, earnings).
Results show a clean card for each school with quick financial stats:
Estimated net price, typical debt, early career earnings, and a simple ROI badge like higher value, average, lower value.

### Screen 2: School Detail
A single school page with:
Key financial snapshot (net price, debt, completion rate, earnings at 10 years).
A simple ROI section that explains the calculation inputs and shows outputs like break even years and estimated value score.
A notes area that clarifies what the numbers mean and where they come from.

### Screen 3: Compare Schools
A side by side table for 2 to 4 schools.
Rows include net price, debt, completion, earnings metrics, and derived metrics like ROI score and break even timeline.
Users can toggle assumptions like:
4 year completion vs 6 year completion
Debt interest rate
Expected earnings growth

### Screen 4: Shortlist
A saved list of schools and comparisons.
Users can add notes like tour impressions or concerns.
Quick actions: open detail, open compare, share.

## Example Scenario

Maya is a high school senior in Delaware trying to decide between University of Delaware and Rutgers.

1. Maya opens College Agent and searches Delaware and New Jersey.
2. She filters to public universities and sets a net price range that fits her family budget.
3. She sees a list of schools with quick stats and taps University of Delaware.
4. On the school detail page, she reviews typical net price, typical debt, and earnings outcomes.
5. She adds UD to her shortlist, then does the same for Rutgers.
6. She opens the Compare Schools screen and selects UD and Rutgers.
7. She sees a side by side comparison plus an ROI estimate and break even timeline for each school.
8. She toggles a setting to compare by major (placeholder for now), and the app explains that major level ROI is a coming feature.
9. She saves the comparison and shares a link with her parents so they can discuss the tradeoffs with the same numbers in front of them.
